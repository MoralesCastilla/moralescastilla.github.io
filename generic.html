Research

My research focuses on understanding and predicting the spatial and temporal large-scale distribution of biodiversity in a context of Climate Change. My work is multi-disciplinary and question-driven; it has emphasized the relevance and timeliness of the question over a focus on a specific study system. Specifically, my most recent research focuses on predicting biodiversity spatial-temporal responses to shifts in climate. To do so, I integrate data and models from the largely disconnected fields of thermal eco-physiology and species phenology. The promising results have the potential to moving disciplines such as macroecology and biogeography towards more applied paradigms.
